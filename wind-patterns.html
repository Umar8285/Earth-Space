Global Wind Patterns and Weather

How Wind Moves Around the Earth:

To be able to understand weather, we have to know how air moves in the Earth's atmosphere. Winds don't just blow randomly and chaotically. Instead, they follow certain patterns that happen all over the planet Earth.

The Wind Patterns:

There are three main types of wind belts in certain regions of the Earth.

  • Trade Winds (Near the Equator)
    • Blow from east to west.
    • They can help form tropical storms and bring rain.
    • These winds are warm and steady.
  • Westerlies (Middle Latitudes)
    • They blow from west to east.
    • They move storms and weather systems across the country.
    • These winds affect the weather in places like the United States.
  • Polar Easterlies (Near the Poles)
    • Blow from east to west.
    • These are cold winds from the North and South Poles.

Reason These Patterns Happen:

  • When the earth spins, it makes the winds curve. This is also called the Coriolis Effect.
  • The sun heats the Earth unevenly. Since the equator is closer to the sun and the poles are further this leads to the equator being hotter than the poles.
  • Warm air rises and cold air sinks. This movement helps create the winds.

Above is diagram explaining the Coriolis Effect

How Wind Can Affect Weather:

  • Winds can move clouds and storms to new places.
  • They can carry warm or cold air into a certain region.
  • They can also spread moisture, which leads to the fall of rain or snow.
