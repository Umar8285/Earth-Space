Weather Basics

What Is Weather?

Weather is what the air around us feels like right now. That can be rain, sun, wind, or snow. It changes from day to day and is comprised of many things—including the atmosphere, the hydosphere, and sunlight.

How the Atmosphere, Hydosphere, and Heat Work Together:

  • The atmosphere is a layer of gases surrounding planet Earth; it contains moisture and moves in currents/patterns.
  • The hydosphere is all of the water on Earth. This includes oceans, rivers, lakes, and clouds (which are water).
  • Heat from the sun increases air, water, and land temperature which causes air to rise, clouds to form, and precipitation/weather to occur.
  • These three elements contribute to the development of precipitation/weather like rain, storms, and snow.

How We Measure Weather:

  • Weather is measured by instruments related to weather:
    • Thermometers (temperature—how warm or cold something is)
    • Barometers (air pressure—indications of storms)
    • Anemometers (wind speed)
    • Rain gauges (precipitation)
    • Hygrometers (humidity).

How Weather Forecasts Are Made

  • Researchers collect weather data from satellites, weather balloons, and ground stations.
  • Computers can analyze historical patterns to predict what will happen.
  • This ability to predict gives researchers the power to fabricate a weather report so that people can be ready for rain, storms, and sunshine!
