Tropical Storms and Hurricanes

How to Track Hurricanes and Stay Safe

Hurricanes are giant storms that form over warm ocean waters. They bring heavy rain, strong winds, and even flooding. Scientists track them with direct and indirect measurements.

  • Direct measurements are sensors on aircraft that fly inside the hurricane to collect wind speed and pressure readings.
  • Indirect measurements are satellites that capture photographs and measure the size and direction of the storm.

With this knowledge, scientists and places such as the National Hurricane Center can know where a hurricane is headed and how powerful it is. This allows people to prepare and be safe.

A Simple School Hurricane Warning System

In order to be safe at school when a hurricane is coming, we can have a warning system that works like this:

  • Watch: If there is a predicted hurricane hit within 48 hours, warning to the students and teachers is issued.
  • Warning: If there is a hurricane approaching earlier, school officials make sure everyone is ready to move to a shelter location.

Easy School Evacuation Plan

How we react if a hurricane is near:

  • Stay Calm – The teachers and students stay calm and hear warnings.
  • Go to the Safe Place – All go to an in-school safe area, like the gym or interior hallway, away from windows.
  • Bring Emergency Supplies – Take flashlights, water, and snacks to the safe place.
  • Stay Up to Date – Keep a radio or phone handy for weather updates.
  • Wait for the All-Clear – Stay in the safe place until it is safe to go home or outside.
